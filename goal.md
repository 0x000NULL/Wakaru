# Wakaru - Project Goals & Vision

## Vision Statement

To create the most comprehensive, research-backed Japanese learning platform that guides complete beginners to fluency through evidence-based methodologies, immersive content, and personalized learning paths—without gamification or artificial engagement tactics.

## Mission

Empower English speakers to achieve genuine Japanese fluency by combining proven language acquisition principles (comprehensible input, spaced repetition, and immersive learning) with modern technology, creating an efficient, effective, and sustainable path to mastery.

## Core Philosophy

### Learning-First Approach
- **No Gamification**: Learning is intrinsically rewarding; we focus on genuine progress over superficial engagement metrics
- **Evidence-Based**: Every feature grounded in language acquisition research (Krashen, SRS studies, immersion methodologies)
- **Comprehensive Coverage**: From first hiragana character to advanced native media comprehension
- **Sustainable Habits**: Build consistent study patterns that lead to long-term retention and fluency

### Japanese-Specific Optimization
- Recognition that Japanese requires ~2,200 hours for English speakers (FSI Category IV language)
- Structured approach to the unique challenges: three writing systems, kanji complexity, grammar patterns
- Emphasis on listening/reading comprehension before production skills
- Integration of cultural context through authentic media

## Target User Personas

### Primary: The Motivated Beginner
**Profile**:
- Complete beginner with no Japanese knowledge
- Age: 16-45, though open to all ages
- Motivated by anime/manga, travel, career, or intellectual challenge
- Willing to commit 30-60 minutes daily
- Seeks structured guidance but values autonomy

**Needs**:
- Clear starting point and progression path
- Understanding of "what to learn when"
- Confidence that they're following best practices
- Measurable progress without artificial rewards
- Transition from structured lessons to immersion

**Journey**:
1. **Weeks 1-2**: Hiragana/Katakana mastery
2. **Months 1-3**: Basic grammar, core vocabulary (500-1000 words), simple reading
3. **Months 3-6**: Kanji introduction, grammar expansion, graded immersion content
4. **Months 6-12**: Vocabulary building (2000+ words), authentic media consumption, sentence mining
5. **Year 2+**: Advanced immersion, JLPT preparation, native content comprehension

### Secondary: The Self-Directed Learner
**Profile**:
- May have some prior exposure
- Frustrated with gamified apps or inefficient methods
- Researched language acquisition theory
- Wants comprehensive tools without hand-holding

**Needs**:
- Flexibility to customize learning path
- Advanced tools (sentence mining, SRS customization)
- Access to all content without artificial gating
- Efficient, data-driven approach

## Learning Objectives

### Stage 1: Foundation (0-3 Months)
**Writing Systems**:
- Master hiragana recognition and typing (100% accuracy)
- Master katakana recognition and typing (100% accuracy)
- Understand when each writing system is used

**Vocabulary**:
- Acquire 500-1000 most frequent words
- Understand words in context through example sentences
- Begin SRS review habit (daily practice)

**Grammar**:
- Comprehend basic sentence structures (は/が particles, です/だ, verb conjugation basics)
- Understand 20-30 fundamental grammar patterns (N5 level)
- Read simple constructed sentences

**Kanji** (Optional in Stage 1):
- Introduction to kanji concept and radicals
- Learn 50-100 most common kanji
- Understand kanji components and building blocks

**Success Criteria**:
- Can read simple Japanese text in hiragana/katakana
- Recognizes 500+ words in context
- Comprehends basic conversations in controlled materials
- Maintains 80%+ SRS review completion rate

### Stage 2: Building Blocks (3-12 Months)
**Vocabulary**:
- Expand to 2000-3000 words (high-frequency + context-based)
- Mine sentences from immersion content
- Develop intuition for word usage patterns

**Kanji**:
- Learn 300-500 kanji with readings and components
- Recognize radicals and use for memory/lookup
- Read kanji in vocabulary context

**Grammar**:
- Master N5-N4 grammar patterns (100+ patterns)
- Transition from explicit study to pattern recognition
- Understand casual vs. formal registers

**Immersion**:
- Begin consuming graded native content (simple anime, children's stories)
- Use interactive subtitles for comprehension support
- Start sentence mining practice

**Success Criteria**:
- JLPT N5 readiness (or achievement)
- Can follow simple anime with Japanese subtitles
- Reads simple manga or graded readers
- Mines 5-10 sentences weekly from immersion

### Stage 3: Immersion & Expansion (Year 2+)
**Vocabulary**:
- Expand to 5000-10,000+ words
- Specialized vocabulary based on interests
- Nuanced understanding of synonyms and register

**Kanji**:
- 1000-2000+ kanji mastery
- Read novels, news, and complex media
- Understand jukugo (compound words) patterns

**Grammar**:
- N3-N1 grammar patterns
- Implicit acquisition through immersion
- Understanding of literary and formal structures

**Immersion**:
- Primary learning activity (2+ hours daily)
- Consume native content without English support
- Sentence mine from diverse sources

**Production**:
- Begin writing practice (journaling, composition)
- Speaking practice (shadowing, conversation)

**Success Criteria**:
- JLPT N3-N1 achievement
- Comfortable with native anime, dramas, novels
- Can comprehend news, articles, and complex content
- Sustainable immersion habit established

## Platform Success Metrics

### Learning Outcomes (Primary)
- **Retention Rate**: % of users still active after 3, 6, 12 months
- **Progression Milestones**: % reaching Stage 1, 2, 3 objectives
- **SRS Consistency**: Average daily review completion rate
- **Immersion Hours**: Average weekly time spent in immersion content
- **JLPT Correlation**: Platform learners' JLPT pass rates vs. general population

### Platform Health (Secondary)
- **User Satisfaction**: NPS score, qualitative feedback
- **Content Coverage**: Vocabulary database size, media library breadth
- **Technical Performance**: Load times, uptime, mobile experience
- **Feature Adoption**: Usage rates of key features (sentence mining, interactive player, etc.)

### Anti-Metrics (What We Don't Optimize For)
- Daily active users (if someone masters hiragana and doesn't need daily practice, that's success)
- Streak counts (consistency matters, not arbitrary daily engagement)
- Time-on-platform (efficient learning is better than prolonged engagement)
- Social sharing/virality (quality learners > quantity of sign-ups)

## Success Definition

The platform succeeds when:

1. **Learning Effectiveness**: Users achieve fluency milestones faster and with better retention than traditional methods
2. **Sustained Engagement**: Users continue studying not because of artificial engagement tactics, but because they're making genuine progress
3. **Community Validation**: Positive outcomes reflected in JLPT pass rates, user testimonials, and organic growth
4. **Scalability**: Platform architecture supports growing user base without degradation
5. **Accessibility**: Available to learners regardless of economic status (through thoughtful pricing/free tier)

## Principles We Don't Compromise

- **No Dark Patterns**: No manipulative engagement tactics, no artificial scarcity, no guilt-tripping
- **Privacy First**: User data used solely for learning optimization, never sold or exploited
- **Accessibility**: Core learning features remain accessible regardless of monetization needs
- **Research-Backed**: New features validated against language acquisition research
- **Quality Over Quantity**: Better to have fewer excellent features than many mediocre ones
